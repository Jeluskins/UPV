About the Project

About the Project

Colombia's War for Independence occurred from 1810 to 1820 with the initial steps to unify against Spanish authority. The events of July 20, 1810 in Santa Fe de Bogota in the Spanish colonial Viceroyalty of New Granada was known as The Colombian Declaration of Independence. They resulted in the establishment of a Junta de Santa Fe that day. The experience in self-government eventually led to the creation of Gran Colombia.

In this website, our group is going to visualize and analyze the dataset of “Women executed during Colombia’s war for independence” from the website Darasketch.com

Plan

Data sorting.

After importing the dataset into Tableau, the tool has automatically set dedicated categories for us according to the existing columns in the file. While importing the dataset in csv. format to Recogita, we could manipulate data reordering based on each column and quickly changed its order based on our need when dealing with them.

Chart making.

Using Tableau, we have created 3 different charts respectively visualizing names, years and places in 3 working sheets. Different colours which were applied to different chunks of data have made a clearer demonstration and have helped us to distinguish facts of each woman easier.

Map creating.

We have created multiple maps in our projects - one based on the dataset and the other based on a merged text file in which the history of Colombia’s war has been described. For the dataset the map has been created faster, the places have been listed clearly in a column. However, the text where contents were obtained from several sources requires more effort, the text analyzing process has been involved here.

Analyzing and results.

Our analysis started from a brief view of our data - the simplest graphic displaying names of executed women in the dataset and the chart showing women who have the same names. The second part of our argument introduced the combination of the visualized dataset and the map generated from the external text we have collected.

Women Executed During the War Period.

Each block in the graphic represents a lady by displaying her name and surname. Opening the graphic in the tool Tableau and hovering the mouse on the block, we get a quick insight of the lady’s name, the date and year when she was executed as well as the place she lost her life.

Conclusion.

Based on the data from the dataset and data from other sources, the project has been processed by interpreting visualized data with 6 defined codes. Although the dataset of “Women executed during Colombia’s war for independence” is respectively small, we have found other external information to support and analyze it. From the basic visualization of the information of women being executed, we got an overall view of who those women were, and realized that while usually males play a dominant role in wars, either because of their physical strength or because of their traditional social positions, women are never absent.
